Setting Priorities

What Are Priorities?

Priorities are things that are more important than others and need to be dealt with first.

They establish the sequence in which things should be done.

★

The Big Rocks of Time

If you fill your jar with sand first, there's no room for the big rocks.

Put the big rocks in first — your most important priorities — then let the smaller things fill in around them.

Utilize a Flow Chart

A flow chart can be used on a daily or weekly basis to ensure that your most important tasks get completed.

It helps you make quick decisions about what to tackle next based on urgency and importance.

Priorities Flow Chart
⇅

The Eisenhower Matrix

Prioritize tasks by urgency and importance

Urgent + Important Do First Crisis, deadlines, emergencies
Not Urgent + Important Schedule Goals, planning, self-care
Urgent + Not Important Delegate Interruptions, some meetings
Not Urgent + Not Important Eliminate Time wasters, distractions

Daily & Weekly Planners

Keeping the day's and week's activities in view can help ensure that priority tasks get accomplished.

It can also help you find free time you didn't know you had!
