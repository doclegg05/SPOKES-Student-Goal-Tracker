    Introduction

    Understanding Interviews

    What is an Interview?

    🤝
    Definition & Purpose
    A structured conversation to assess mutual fit, skills, and readiness for a job role.
    💼
    Employer Perspective
    Employers evaluate whether candidates align with company culture and job requirements.
    🎯
    Candidate Perspective
    Candidates showcase qualifications and assess whether the organization fits their career goals.
    📱
    Interview Formats
    Interviews can be conducted in-person, by phone, video, or in panel formats.

    Interviewing is a Skill

    Practice Enhances Skill

    Skills improve with mock interviews and self-reflection, building confidence and effectiveness.

    Preparation Reduces Anxiety

    Proper preparation significantly reduces the natural nervousness of high-stakes interviews.

    Learning from Feedback

    Using rubrics and peer reviews helps candidates refine their approach and meet expectations.

    Simulating Real Scenarios

    Simulated interview environments build competence and readiness for actual job interviews.

    The Interview Process

    Five key phases of a successful interview

    1
    Preparation Phase
    Research the employer, review the job description, and practice responses.
    2
    Beginning of Interview
    Start with greetings and introductions to set a positive tone.
    3
    Core Discussion
    Engage in the main conversation: questions asked and detailed answers given.
    4
    Closing
    Wrap up with final questions and a strong expression of interest.
    5
    Follow-Up
    Send a thank-you note and reflect on the experience.

    "Every interview is a chance to grow. The more you practice, the more confident you become."

    Preparing for the Interview

    Preparation Tips

    1
    Research the Employer
    Learn the company's mission, culture, and recent news to show genuine interest.
    2
    Review the Job Description
    Study requirements carefully to align your skills and tailor responses.
    3
    Practice Interview Questions
    Rehearsing common questions improves confidence and communication.
    4
    Plan Attire & Arrival
    Choosing professional clothing and arriving early demonstrate professionalism.

    Researching the Employer

    🏢
    Understand the Company
    • Learn the company's purpose, goals, products, and services.
    • Review their website, mission statement, and recent news.
    • Know who they serve and what makes them unique.
    🌐
    Learn Company Culture
    • Familiarize yourself with values to align responses during the interview.
    • Look at employee reviews and social media presence.
    • Understand team structure and workplace expectations.
    ⭐
    Demonstrate Initiative
    • Employers are impressed by candidates who show genuine interest through research.
    • Reference specific company achievements or news in your answers.
    • Ask informed questions that show you did your homework.

    Walk in prepared.
    Walk out confident.

    Interview Techniques & Follow-Up

    Answering Questions: The STAR Method

    A structured approach for behavioral interview questions

    S
    Situation
    Describe the specific situation you faced. Give enough detail for context.
    T
    Task
    Explain what you needed to accomplish and your specific role in that situation.
    A
    Action
    Describe what YOU did. Use past tense — tell what you did, not what you would do.
    R
    Result
    Share the outcome. What happened? What did you accomplish? What did you learn?

    STAR in Action: Handling Stress

    Interviewer Question

    "Tell me about a time you faced a stressful situation that demonstrated your coping ability."

    S-T

    I had worked five 12-hour shifts in a row and was asked to stay when we were slammed with urgent exam requests.

    A

    I agreed to stay and we worked as a team, prioritizing the most critical exams first.

    R

    We turned a stressful situation into a manageable one — patients were well cared for and pleased with our service.

    STAR in Action: Upset Customer

    Interviewer Question

    "Tell me about a situation in which you had to deal with a very upset customer or co-worker."

    S-T

    I was working in a nursing home when a patient's very upset family member told me the room hadn't been cleaned in 2 days.

    A

    I reassured the family member it would be handled, reported to my supervisor, and a housekeeper was assigned immediately.

    R

    The family was pleased with our quick response and the patient was well taken care of.

    Questions to Ask the Interviewer

    Always come prepared with thoughtful questions — it shows genuine interest.

    1
    Will you describe the characteristics of the person best suited for this job?
    2
    What advancement opportunities are available with your company?
    3
    What type of people tend to do well in this position — and who does not?
    4
    Are there any reservations about my fit for this position I could try to address?
    ★
    Must Ask: When will you be making a decision and how will I be notified?

    During & Closing the Interview

    ☕
    While You're There
    • Maintain good posture and stay positive throughout.
    • Organize thoughts before answering each question.
    • Refer to your resume or portfolio when appropriate.
    • Express willingness to learn and grow.
    👍
    Wrapping Up
    • Ask your prepared questions.
    • Thank the interviewer sincerely.
    • Smile and shake hands confidently.
    • Express continued interest in the role.
    📝
    Follow-Up Etiquette
    • Send a thank-you note within 24 hours.
    • Reflect on strengths and areas to improve.
    • Follow up politely after one week if no response.
